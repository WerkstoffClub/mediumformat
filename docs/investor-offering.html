Section03 · Package

Why one package. Not six line items.

Package · Indivisible

The modules aren't independent products — they share one database, one auth layer, one design system and one build. Split them and the shop stops being a shop. The offering is priced accordingly.

A shop needs to sell everywhere at once from one truth of stock. Pull any module and something visible to the buyer breaks.
Package Thesis · Medium Format Build
01 · Compounding value

Every module lifts the others

POS sales top up marketplace stock in real time. Newsletters promote new arrivals from the same catalog. Reports read every channel. Value grows with each connection, not in isolation.

02 · One codebase

Shared foundations, priced once

Auth, roles, data model, design tokens, jobs queue and deploy pipeline are built once and reused six times. Splitting the package doesn't save money — it duplicates the foundation.

03 · One truth of stock

Stock isn't a feature — it's the axis

Every module reads & writes the same inventory. Selling from POS while Tokopedia is offline, or missing a wantlist, is a business failure — not a de-scope.

// If you unbundle · what breaks
Skip POS Split stock
In-store sales stop decrementing website stock. Online shoppers buy records that were sold at the counter an hour earlier. Chargebacks and refunds follow.
Skip Newsletter Rent your audience
You keep paying Meta and Tokopedia to reach buyers you've already converted. First-party newsletter is the only channel that survives platform pricing changes.
Skip Marketplaces Miss 60% of demand
Indonesian vinyl demand lives on Tokopedia, Shopee and Discogs. Website-only means a store that only serves the buyers who already know the brand.
Skip Reports Fly blind on tax
PPN 11% and channel profitability aren't optional — they decide which titles you re-order. Without unified reports, restocking becomes guesswork.
Skip Back Office Manual everything
Every order, message, and label goes back to spreadsheets and screenshots. Staff cost balloons faster than the software you declined to build.
