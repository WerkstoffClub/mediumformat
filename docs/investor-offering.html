Section02 · Why Now

Three forces make own software urgent. Right now.

Strategy · 2026

Every extra year the shop operates on rented platforms and off-the-shelf counter software, more margin leaks and more customer data belongs to someone else. These are the three forces investors should weigh.

Force 01 · Fees

Marketplace fees keep rising in Indonesia

Tokopedia and Shopee raised seller commissions across 2024–2025. Every rupiah of margin now competes with the platform first, other sellers second. Own site takes the transaction directly.

Per Rp 500,000 sale Rp 32,500 lost to Toped commission + payment fee.
Own site (Xendit QRIS ≈ 0.7%): only Rp 3,500 lost.
Force 02 · Funnel

Instagram & Facebook Marketplace are dark funnels

Buyers discover on IG or FB Marketplace, then DM to ask about price and stock. Payment ends up as a screenshot & bank transfer photo. No cart, no email captured, no retargeting.

The redirect Every IG / FB click should land on a Medium Format product page — cart, checkout, and customer captured on your domain, not in a DM thread.
Force 03 · Ops

Off-the-shelf POS splits the shop in half

Moka, Pawoon and similar counter apps run their own catalog, their own stock, their own customer list. None of it talks to the website or marketplaces. Every count, every price is done twice.

Reality check A record sold at the counter at 14:00 can be sold again on Tokopedia at 14:03. Refund, apology, review damage — every week, until the two systems become one.
Bottom line
Every marketplace fee hike, every Instagram DM inquiry, and every counter transaction that never touches the website is margin the shop is quietly leaking — plus data the shop never gets to own. The build stops the leak.
Sources for fee data: public Tokopedia & Shopee seller-fee schedules 2024–2025. Xendit QRIS pricing: xendit.co / pricing.

Why it's more secure
Your VPS, your Postgres, your rules.

Every record — sales, stock movements, customer PII — lives on your VPS, not on a vendor's shared cloud. TLS end-to-end (Let's Encrypt), Auth.js JWT sessions, per-role RBAC on every write, and a full audit log on stock, price and order changes. Nightly encrypted Postgres backups to object storage.

AI-assisted purchase orders
One draft PO per distributor, every morning.

Overnight job reads counter sell-through, website demand and marketplace inquiries. Aggregates by title & distributor, then drafts a consolidated PO ready to send — the shop replaces guessing with signal. Something off-the-shelf POS can't do, because off-the-shelf POS can't see your website.

Push · Listings
Every arrival posts everywhere in one job.

One "publish" in the back office pushes the listing to Discogs, Tokopedia and Shopee with the right per-channel price, image, description and shipping profile. AI copy assist (see feature 06) drafts marketplace-safe titles per platform.

Pull · Orders & Stock
Webhooks in. Reconciler nightly.

Every marketplace sale posts a webhook that decrements the shared stock counter in seconds. An overnight reconciler cross-checks each channel's inventory API vs Postgres and flags drift on a sync-health dashboard.

// If this feature is dropped
Skip cost
The shop lists on marketplaces manually — the way it does today.

Every price change happens 4 times. Every arrival is photographed and typed 4 times. When something sells in the shop, staff have 30 minutes to unlist it in three other places before someone else buys it. Marketplaces account for a majority of Indonesian vinyl discovery — dropping the sync doesn't drop the workload, it redistributes it to staff hours.

Why owned audience
The one channel platform pricing can't touch.

Toped and Shopee decide who sees your listings. Instagram decides what part of a post the buyer sees. Your newsletter list is the only channel where reach doesn't depend on someone else's algorithm — or their price hike.

Wholesale segment built in
A separate list, a separate price.

The wholesale role (from feature 03) segments the list automatically. Wholesale buyers get their own newsletter with role-priced records; retail never sees the trade prices.
