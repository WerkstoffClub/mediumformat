01·About Medium Format

Records, the way
they should
be sold.

Medium Format is an independent record shop in Jakarta. The name comes from film photography, where the medium format is the larger negative that holds more detail. It also points to what we sell: the LP, the 12-inch, the 7-inch and the CD.

We buy records carefully and sell them everywhere our customers shop: in the shop, on the website, and on the marketplaces they already use. One platform sits behind all of it and keeps stock, orders and music in sync, so the admin stays out of the way.

The identity matches how the shop works. It is plain and modern: black and white, one typeface, and a single mark drawn from a record seen side-on. Nothing is loud, and nothing is added for the sake of it.

02·How the brand should feel

Plain, and
sure of itself.

Two things make up the brand: a plain wordmark and a quiet black-and-white system. The character is in the words we choose and the restraint we keep, not in decoration. Let the records and the cover art be the most interesting thing on the page.

Plain

Clear words, clean layouts, no clutter. If something can come out, take it out.

Confident

Say things once and mean them. No hype, no exclamation marks doing the work.

Quiet

The records are the interest. The design stays in the background and lets them lead.

Honest

Describe what is real: the pressing, the condition, the price. Nothing dressed up.

05·Clear space

Give it room.

Keep a clear margin around the logo so nothing crowds it. The margin is at least X on every side, where X is the cap height of the wordmark.

Nothing sits inside this margin: not text, an image edge, a fold, or another logo.

09·Colour

Black on white,
white on black.

That contrast is the whole palette. Keeping to it is what makes the brand feel like one thing across the shop, the website and print.

Colour has one job in the product: showing the state of stock and orders. Those status colours are working signals only. They never appear in the logo or in marketing.

12·How we write

Talk like
the shop.

Write the way good staff talk: clear, friendly and to the point. We work in Indonesian and English, and prices are shown in Rupiah first.

Do
  • Lead with the record and why it is worth hearing.
  • Be specific about the label, year and condition.
  • Keep it short. One good line is plenty.
Don’t
  • Oversell, or invent scarcity to push a sale.
  • Use jargon that shuts newcomers out.
  • Pad sentences with filler.
Instead of“INSANE restock!! Don’t sleep on this absolute heater, link in bio.”
We’d say“Back in stock: Khruangbin, Mordechai. A few copies on the wall, come and have a listen.”

13·Photography and imagery

Let the records
be the colour.

Pictures should feel real and unfussy: records, sleeves, the shop, hands going through a crate. Plain light and honest framing. The cover art brings the colour, so the layout around it stays neutral.

What to shoot

Records and sleeves, the shop and its shelves, the turntable, people listening. Real moments over staged ones.

How it should look

Clean and a little understated. Black and white works for the shop itself; let the sleeves carry the colour.

A simple test: would it sit happily on a record sleeve or a gig poster? If it looks like a tech advert, take it back a step.
